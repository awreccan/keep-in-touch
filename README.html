The idea

Relationships fade not from a decision, but from inattention. Keep In Touch answers one question, continuously and without guilt: who have I been neglecting? — then makes reaching out a single tap.

How it works

Keep In Touch is a renderer, not a data owner. It holds no shared bucket of your data — your data lives in your storage, and the app just renders and writes it back.

1
Google Sheet (recommended)The Sheet is your database — open it, click a cell, it autosaves. The app reads/writes it as JSON via a tiny Apps Script. One Sheet = many projects. This is what syncs phone ↔ computer.
2
Connected local file (desktop)Bind to a .json you choose; edits write straight to disk and auto-reconnect next launch.
3
Browser storage (fallback / offline)Namespaced per project; doubles as the offline cache.

🔒 Private by design. The hosted URL (awreccan.github.io/keep-in-touch) is code only — it contains no data. Your contacts never leave your Google account or device. Updating the app never loses your data: it's keyed by project, not by the HTML. Isolate lists with ?project=family, ?project=work, etc. Installable as a PWA on Android & macOS.
